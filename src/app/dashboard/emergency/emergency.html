Emergency

Choking

Steps to follow:

  1. 1. Stay calm and assess the situation
  2. 2. For infants: 5 back blows, then 5 chest thrusts
  3. 3. For toddlers: Heimlich maneuver - upward abdominal thrusts
  4. 4. Continue until object is expelled or child becomes unconscious
  5. 5. If unconscious, begin CPR and call 911 immediately
‚ö†Ô∏è

Call 911 if:

Unable to remove object, child becomes unconscious, or turns blue

Allergic Reaction

Steps to follow:

  1. 1. Identify and remove the allergen if possible
  2. 2. Give antihistamine (Benadryl) if available and age-appropriate
  3. 3. Monitor breathing and consciousness
  4. 4. Use EpiPen if prescribed and symptoms are severe
  5. 5. Keep child calm and in comfortable position

Call 911 if:

Difficulty breathing, swelling of face/throat, severe rash, or loss of consciousness

Injury

Cuts and Bleeding

Steps to follow:

  1. 1. Apply direct pressure with clean cloth
  2. 2. Elevate the injured area above heart level if possible
  3. 3. Do not remove embedded objects
  4. 4. Clean around wound with water (not hydrogen peroxide)
  5. 5. Cover with sterile bandage
  6. 6. Monitor for signs of infection

Call 911 if:

Bleeding won't stop after 10 minutes of pressure, deep cuts, or spurting blood
